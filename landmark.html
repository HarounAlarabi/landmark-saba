Landmarks of Saba

A Kingdom of Wealth and Engineering

The Ancient Kingdom of Saba, centered in modern-day Yemen, rose to prominence as a dominant force on the Arabian Peninsula from the 1st millennium BCE. Its immense wealth was built on controlling the lucrative Incense Route, trading precious frankincense and myrrh to the Roman, Greek, and Egyptian empires.

The Sabaeans were master hydrologists and engineers. Their capital, Marib, flourished in the desert thanks to the monumental Great Dam of Marib. This marvel of engineering, one of the sites below, irrigated over 25,000 acres, creating a legendary oasis that supported a large, centralized population for centuries.

The Legacy of the Queen of Sheba

Saba is perhaps most famous for the legendary figure known as the Queen of Sheba (called Bilqis in Islamic tradition). Her celebrated journey to visit King Solomon in Jerusalem, as chronicled in the Bible, Quran, and other texts, highlights the kingdom's international reputation for wisdom, gold, and exotic goods. While shrouded in legend, her story symbolizes the power and cultural significance of the Sabaean kingdom at its zenith.
